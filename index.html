November 
2021

Dear Elizabeth Academy Families,

Classroom Halloween celebrations were a success! The students and teachers had a wonderful time dressing up, playing games, and eating treats. We appreciate the room parents and volunteers who helped to make these class parties fun and memorable. Because we missed coming together as a school community for our costume parade, we will send you photos of some highlights of our day.

November brings Elizabeth Academy’s tradition of celebrating family and our elders, community and gratitude, freedom and our veterans. Though we must put our annual Gratitude Feast on hold this year, we have found other ways to celebrate and focus on these school values. In honor of Veterans Day on November 11th, teachers will lead discussions and activities about people who have served our country in the military. Students will have the opportunity to write letters and draw pictures to be distributed to veterans in assisted living facilities in our city. On November 23rd, the students will assemble hygiene kits for people living at Palmer Court, a permanent, supportive housing complex run by the Road Home. Teachers and students will learn about and discuss homelessness in age appropriate ways. To continue the conversation at home, here are some tips and information.

The Road Home

How to Talk With Kids about Homelessness

As I reflect upon the things I am grateful for, our beautiful school community is near the top of my list. Thank you for your smiles and kind words, your support of your child and our school’s mission, and for being our partner in developing a better way to educate our children. Happy Thanksgiving!

Gratefully,
Jessica Rawlings, Head of School

 
Merit Scholarships
 
Good news! Merit Scholarships for EA/GS Upper Elementary and Secondary students are available. Our current 3rd, 4th and 5th graders can apply for Upper Elementary and our 6th graders can apply for Secondary scholarships. The areas of qualification are: academic performance, citizenship/leadership, inclusivity, and financial need. Each of these areas have a monetary amount that can be awarded. A student may be awarded the money for one area or all areas. The applications are due by November 30th and the scholarships will be awarded before Winter Break. Please follow this link for more information about these scholarships. Students should submit their applications to Makyla Ordonez. (makyla@elizabethacademy.com)

Infant and Toddler Update

The Infant and Toddler Halloween parties were very memorable and filled with big smiles, treats, activities, and games! In November, we will be exploring fun topics such as corn, vegetables, gratitude, and holiday traditions. 

 

Early Childhood Update

Another month has come and gone, just like the changing of seasons. November will be full of great lessons taught at the EC level! We’ll study the United States, learn about our flag, landmarks, Utah, Native Americans, community helpers, and Thanksgiving traditions. Other important topics will be displaying what gratitude, family, and heritage signify for us. We’ll be sending letters of thanks to the Veterans who help protect and serve our country and come together to make hygiene kits for the homeless. Botany brings discussions of harvest, corn, and one of our favorite lessons—Stone Soup! We’re excited to work together to help our community.

Lower Elementary Update

The Lower Elementary students have been enjoying their study of Oceania. In our lessons, we have been talking about the countries of Australia and New Zealand, along with many other island nations found in the Pacific regions of Polynesia, Micronesia, and Melanesia. Students are learning about the landforms, flags, customs, animals, and foods characteristic to this continent.

Upper Elementary Update

We are excited to welcome Alice Pope to UE1 as a lead teacher. 4th grade students will continue their study on ecology. The 5th grade students will finish up their study of the human body. And our 6th graders will continue the study of the tree of life and evolution of groups of species.
